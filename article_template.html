Multiomics Analysis

Multiomics analysis refers to the integration of data from multiple omics technologies, such as genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and metabolomics, to provide a more comprehensive view of biological systems. By analyzing multiple omics datasets simultaneously, researchers can gain a better understanding of complex biological processes and identify new biomarkers or therapeutic targets.

Network Theory in Biology

Network theory provides a powerful framework for analyzing complex biological systems, such as gene regulatory networks or protein-protein interaction networks. By representing biological entities as nodes and their interactions as edges, network analysis can reveal key nodes or modules that are critical for system function or disease states. Network analysis can also be combined with other omics data, such as gene expression or metabolite profiles, to provide a more comprehensive view of biological systems.
